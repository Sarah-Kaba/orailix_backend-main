Palaiseau, France – November 30, 2024 – The LIX research lab is excited to announce an upcoming seminar titled "Fuzzing for Vulnerability Detection with an Outlook on AI-Driven Software Analysis," to be held in January 2024 - exact date to be announced soon. The seminar will be led by Dr. Konstantin Böttinger, Head of Department Cognitive Security Technologies at the Fraunhofer Institute for Applied and Integrated Security (AISEC).

Dr. Böttinger brings over 13 years of experience in transforming applied research into high-tech security solutions. His seminar will focus on innovative testing methods to enhance software security, combining probability theory with current testing technologies. The talk will also provide insights into AI-driven software analysis, offering a comprehensive view of the future of cybersecurity.

Seminar Details:

  • Date: Jan, 2024 - exact date to be announced soon
  • Time: Morning session (exact time to be confirmed)
  • Venue: LIX, 1 rue Honoré d'Etienne d'Orves, 91128 Palaiseau, France
  • Speaker: Dr. Konstantin Böttinger, MBA, Fraunhofer AISEC

About the Speaker:

Dr. Konstantin Böttinger is heading the Department Cognitive Security Technologies at Fraunhofer AISEC. He has 13 years of experience in transforming applied research into high-tech security solutions for various industries. His research focuses on the intersection of artificial intelligence and cybersecurity. He completed his PhD at Technical University Munich and an MBA at the University of Oxford. Prior to joining Fraunhofer AISEC, Konstantin Böttinger studied mathematics and physics at Heidelberg University.
